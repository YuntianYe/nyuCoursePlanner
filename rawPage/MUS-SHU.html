Course Location: Shanghai | Component: Grp Instr in the Perform Arts
09/04/2017 - 12/15/2017 Thu 5.30 PM - 8.30 PM at PDNG 828 with Mirrione, James
Notes: Course Repeatable for Credit. Course: Private Lives: An Interdisciplinary Arts Project The aim of this course is to recreate Noel Cowards play Private Lives in the very setting in which the author composed the work in 1930. Using original music from Cowards repertoire, dances of the era and by staging various scenes in the Jasmine Lounge, which contains much of the original dcor of the Thirties, this production seeks to evoke the artistic atmosphere that is equally matched by the timelessness of the Peace Hotel and the classic appeal of one of Cowards most famous plays. This course also affords ample opportunities for NYUSH students to extend their acting and music training to an environment which will allow them to perform for the Shanghai public, as not only a contribution to the community but also as evidence of one of the collaborative and outreach objectives of NYUSH. The modality of interactive media arts will also bring an innovative enhancement to both the play and to the hotel by use of projections, lighting and sound that incorporates the foyer, lounge, and staircases into a new visual and auditory setting. It is also envisioned that the ballroom dancers, currently engaged by the Peace Hotel as part of their weekend brunch Tea Dance, will also the join the student performers in a series of shows scheduled for November and December, 2017. Therefore, this class will study, rehearse and present Noel Cowards Private Lives, originating first at NYU Shanghai, and in 2-3 other venues in Shanghai toward the end of the Fall semester, 2017. However, the penultimate performance space will be at the historic Peace Hotel on The Bund in Old Shanghai. Students will study the work, the author and its connections to Shanghai in the decade of the 1930s. Each student will have a specific task be it as an actor, singer or part of the production team in fully realizing this piece, as a final project class and as performing ensemble for NYU Shanghai and for the greater Shanghai community at large. *Some additional rehearsal at mutually convenient times may be required as we get closer to the initial performance date. Those interested in participating should contact the instructor below. 7 cast members 5 production and stage crew Students interested in the above should contact Prof. Mirrione: (jm24@nyu.edu) 3 singers Students interested should contact Prof. Heldman: (dianna.heldman@nyu.edu)
