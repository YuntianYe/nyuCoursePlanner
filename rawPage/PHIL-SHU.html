In this course, we shall survey a range of topics in Indian Buddhist philosophy. Using translations of primary texts, we will pay attention to arguments that Buddhist philosophers used to defend their views and respond to their critics. Our approach to these arguments shall be twofold. On the one hand, we will try to understand these arguments in their historical context. On the other hand, we will ask what we, as philosophers, can learn from these arguments. The aim of this course is to familiarize students with prominent questions, arguments, and views in classical Indian metaphysics, epistemology, philosophy of mind, ethics, and philosophy of religion.
